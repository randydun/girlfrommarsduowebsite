Meet the Band

Girl From Mars

It was an inevitable collision of cosmic interest in great songs that brought Jennifer Vazquez and Randy Dunham together. Though both from Earth, their sound together is clearly from another planet — or perhaps Donelson. Who’s to say?

Drawing from five decades of beloved music, Girl From Mars performs an eclectic acoustic mix of radio hits. From heartfelt ballads like I Can’t Make You Love Me and Best of My Love to crowd-pleasing singalongs like Don’t Stop Believin’ and When Will I Be Loved, the duo delivers an evening of familiar songs, rich harmonies, and memorable performances.

Whether entertaining in restaurants, breweries, private events, or intimate listening rooms, Girl From Mars brings warmth, energy, and a touch of interplanetary charm to every stage.

Jennifer Vazquez

Born and raised in The Bronx, Jennifer Vazquez is a sixteen-time ASCAPlu$ Award winner whose captivating voice and dynamic stage presence have earned audiences throughout the United States and abroad.

Her musical style blends pop, folk-rock, and country influences, and she has toured extensively as a solo artist on both coasts as well as internationally throughout Sweden and England. As one half of the acclaimed duo Stilettos-n-Steel with Jason Waters, Jennifer performed at wineries, corporate events, and premier venues for enthusiastic audiences nationwide.

A seven-year residence in Los Angeles is when she honed and strengthened her skills performing on the 3rd Street Promenade in Santa Monica. She relocated to Nashville in 2016, where she has appeared live on Lightning 100, was a finalist in 2015 Tennessee Songwriter’s Week, and for three years has hosted monthly open mics at The Bowery Vault in East Nashville.

With lyrical and musical sprinkles of Linda Perry, Patty Griffin, and Jennifer Nettles, Jen’s influences and inspirations started with Donna Summer, Billy Joel, and Joni Mitchell. She has recorded in New Orleans, Los Angeles, Austin, and Nashville.

Randy Dunham

A graduate of both Musicians Institute and UCLA, Randy has spent a lifetime exploring diverse musical styles, from hair metal and emo to pop, country, and acoustic music.

As co-founder of the band Brokedown Cadillac with Corri English, Randy enjoyed success with more than thirty film and television placements, including Desperate Housewives, The Mentalist, and Disney’s Race to Witch Mountain. The group also completed three overseas tours performing for U.S. troops stationed in Kuwait and Iraq.

Randy has long been a fixture in the acoustic music scene, performing in numerous duos throughout his career. Most notably, he spent ten years with Radio Flyer, a Saturday-night staple at the world-famous Burbank Bar & Grille. His passion for great songs remains as strong as ever — largely because there are still so many left to learn.

A dyslexic, Randy enjoys hot beer and cold women…
